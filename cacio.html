Cacio e pepe

Description

Cacio e pepe is a pasta dish typical of the Lazio region of Italy. Cacio e pepe means 'cheese and pepper' in several central Italian dialects. In keeping with its name, the dish contains grated pecorino romano and black pepper, together with tonnarelli or spaghetti.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb spaghetti
  • 6 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 3/4 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti in boiling water with occasional stirring until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of cooking water and drain the spaghetti.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large frying pan over medium heat. Cook and stir garlic and black pepper in the hot oil until fragrant, about 1 to 2 minutes. Add cooked spaghetti and Pecorino Romano cheese. Ladle in 1/2 cup of the cooking water. Stir until the cheese has melted and coated the spaghetti. Stir in more cooking water as needed, 1 tbsp at a time.
  3. Serve pretentiously with a carving fork.
